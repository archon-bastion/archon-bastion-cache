Setania



I dreamt of a dormant village,

Situated in the wooded region of a great world. Annular chain of clouds encircling a cobalt moon overhead. I dreamt of the deafening joy within an enclosed star, swallowed by the charcoal-silted clutches of a black hole. Trailing rainbow-streams entwined with pentacles of colored light; immolated, wreathed in flame. The last sparks of a sun reduced to embers; faint as a single thread of gossamer, vestiges of a once-smoldering vitality.

Structure emerges from the anticipatory; embedded in the release of a held breath. In the moments before a book is closed, the vowels of memory espouse the gestures of the past. The rhythm of life is slower than the pulse of blood, yet faster than the candle's wane. In the lacuna of a moment's irresolution, we are to be made whole or undone by a quickened heart.

So dream, dream, dream. Wash away the blood, wash away the dirt. Look, at all that men have wrought. Wash your hands in the river; her specious stream-waters languish on. Coy bed veiled below, here brutish boulders render formless, and even the roughest icons weather her mute touch not. Violent figures dispersed as the seeds of a new spring. With each season's quiet approach comes a gradual; but sure, erasure of the past. Even as her vague shores swirl with the faintest traces of memory.

Only within sleep's rich and mysterious fiefdom, can thoughts of vexation be swept away into airy oblivion. A petrified instant: terminal tress of an infinite braid, cleaved-off sliver of chaos. The grand spiral stilled by the eye of Setania, to be rewoven and made anew.

But, I dream of her as she was. Streams of silver through my hands, weaving me a gown of aqueous thread, a silken curtain interlaced with gold. Blue muslin ribbons in my hair and lilies scattered at my feet, I am dressed in the gilded raiment of the primal dream. The slowest of agonies against a bitter caress; upon me the world is gathered. Whispers left in the dark, a timeless grief, the utter resignation of the past. When all is still and empty of thought, what then is that which observes the primal stirrings, as pensive shadows crawl across the illumined. The grandeur of a landscape no longer marred. - Walls of ancient stone now dust, now lost. Along the river's bank, a river's dust shall I be, and a river's dream.

So dream, dream, dream. Wash away the blood, wash away the dirt. Look, at all that men have wrought. Wash your hands in the river; her specious stream-waters languish on. For one day soon all will be made new; in the crescent glow of fading rose-light and falling snow. A time of spring will come to you once more; like warmth returning to an old friend's smile.
